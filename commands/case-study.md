# /case-study

Write a compelling customer case study in the challenge-solution-results format with a strong narrative arc, quotable metrics, and persuasive storytelling.

## What This Command Does

This command creates a complete customer case study that transforms a customer success story into a persuasive marketing asset. It follows the proven challenge-solution-results structure while weaving in narrative elements that make the story relatable and memorable. The output includes a compelling headline, customer snapshot, the full case study narrative, pull quotes, a metrics sidebar, and recommendations for how to distribute and repurpose the story. The case study is designed to work across formats — as a standalone PDF, website page, sales enablement asset, and source material for social content.

## When to Use

- Documenting a customer success story for your website or resource library
- Creating sales enablement content that proves ROI
- Building credibility for a specific use case, industry, or company size
- Supporting a product launch with real-world validation
- Strengthening a proposal with relevant proof points
- Generating content for social media, email, and conference presentations
- Building a library of proof points organized by industry, use case, or outcome

## How It Works

1. **Gathers the story** — Asks about the customer, their challenge, your solution, and the measurable results
2. **Identifies the narrative angle** — Finds the most compelling thread that ties the story together
3. **Structures the case study** — Builds the challenge-solution-results framework with clear sections
4. **Writes the narrative** — Creates engaging prose that balances storytelling with specificity
5. **Crafts quotes** — Writes natural-sounding customer quotes that capture key moments and outcomes
6. **Highlights metrics** — Surfaces the most impressive numbers and presents them for maximum impact
7. **Creates the summary** — Builds a quick-scan version with headline stats for busy readers
8. **Plans distribution** — Recommends how to use the case study across channels and formats

The command delegates to the **content-strategist** and **copywriter** agents, combining strategic narrative structure with polished writing.

## Important Notes

- **Real numbers are everything.** A case study without specific, quantified results is just a testimonial. Push for exact metrics — percentages, dollar amounts, time saved, team size changes.
- **Customer approval is required.** The agent writes the case study, but you must get written approval from the customer before publishing. Share the draft with them and incorporate their feedback.
- **Quotes should sound like real people.** The generated quotes avoid corporate-speak and are designed to sound conversational. When possible, replace them with actual quotes from customer interviews.
- **The challenge section is the most important.** Readers need to see themselves in the customer's problem before they care about your solution. Invest time making the challenge vivid and relatable.
- **Avoid making it a product feature list.** The best case studies focus on the customer's transformation, not on your feature set. The product is the mechanism, not the hero.
- **One case study, many assets.** A single well-crafted case study can generate 10+ pieces of content across channels. The distribution plan helps you extract maximum value.
