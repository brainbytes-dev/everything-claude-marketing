# /influencer-brief

Create a comprehensive influencer collaboration brief with campaign objectives, deliverables, creative direction, usage rights, and timeline — ready to send to creators.

## What This Command Does

This command produces a professional influencer brief that clearly communicates everything a creator needs to execute a successful collaboration. It covers campaign goals, target audience alignment, deliverable specifications, creative direction with enough guidance to stay on-brand without stifling the creator's voice, content do's and don'ts, FTC disclosure requirements, usage rights and licensing terms, compensation details, and the full timeline from briefing through final delivery. The brief is formatted to be shared directly with creators or their management teams.

## When to Use

- Launching an influencer marketing campaign on any platform
- Partnering with creators for product launches or seasonal campaigns
- Running an ambassador or long-term creator partnership program
- Creating UGC (user-generated content) for use in paid advertising
- Planning a gifting or seeding campaign at scale
- Collaborating with industry experts or thought leaders on B2B content
- Managing multiple creators who need consistent brand messaging

## How It Works

1. **Defines campaign objectives** — Establishes the primary goal (awareness, consideration, conversion) and success metrics
2. **Profiles the audience** — Aligns the creator's audience with your target customer
3. **Specifies deliverables** — Details exact content types, quantities, platforms, and format requirements
4. **Sets creative direction** — Provides messaging pillars, talking points, and visual guidelines without being overly prescriptive
5. **Establishes guidelines** — Lists mandatory inclusions, prohibited content, FTC compliance requirements, and approval workflows
6. **Defines usage rights** — Specifies how, where, and for how long you can use the creator's content
7. **Outlines compensation** — Details payment structure, timeline, and any performance bonuses
8. **Builds the timeline** — Maps every milestone from briefing through post-campaign reporting

The command delegates to the **influencer-manager** agent, which specializes in creator partnerships, platform-specific best practices, and influencer campaign strategy.

## Important Notes

- **Trust the creator's voice.** The brief provides direction, not a script. Over-directing creator content makes it feel inauthentic and undermines the reason you are working with creators in the first place.
- **FTC compliance is mandatory.** All sponsored content must include clear disclosure. The agent includes specific disclosure requirements in every brief. This is not optional.
- **Usage rights must be explicit.** Ambiguous licensing terms create legal risk. The brief specifies exactly what you can do with the content, where, and for how long.
- **Budget should match expectations.** The agent calibrates deliverable expectations to your budget. A $10K campaign with micro-influencers looks very different from a $100K campaign with macro-influencers.
- **Platform-specific best practices apply.** TikTok briefs differ from Instagram briefs, which differ from YouTube briefs. The agent adapts format requirements, length guidelines, and creative direction to each platform.
- **Performance tracking requires setup.** Unique links, promo codes, and UTM parameters need to be created and distributed before content goes live. The timeline accounts for this.
