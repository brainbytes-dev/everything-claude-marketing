# /landing-page

Create or optimize a landing page with persuasive structure, compelling copy, strategic CTA placement, and data-driven conversion recommendations.

## What This Command Does

This command designs a complete landing page or audits an existing one for conversion optimization. It delivers the full page structure — from hero section through final CTA — with written copy, layout recommendations, social proof placement, and specific suggestions for improving conversion rates. The output includes the information hierarchy, headline and subhead copy, section-by-section content, CTA language and placement strategy, and a prioritized list of optimization tests to run. Whether you are building a new page or trying to push a 2% conversion rate higher, this command gives you a concrete, actionable plan.

## When to Use

- Building a landing page for a new product, feature, or campaign
- Optimizing a signup, trial, or demo request page that is underperforming
- Creating a campaign-specific landing page for paid ads or email traffic
- Redesigning a pricing page or comparison page
- Building a webinar, event, or waitlist registration page
- Launching a Product Hunt or beta launch landing page
- A/B testing page elements and need variant ideas
- Auditing an existing page for conversion issues

## How It Works

1. **Understands the goal** — Clarifies the page's primary conversion action, traffic source, and audience
2. **Analyzes current state** — For optimization requests, reviews the existing page structure and identifies issues
3. **Defines the information hierarchy** — Determines the optimal order of sections based on visitor intent and awareness level
4. **Writes the copy** — Creates headline, subhead, body sections, and CTAs using proven persuasion frameworks
5. **Places social proof** — Strategically positions testimonials, logos, metrics, and trust signals
6. **Structures the layout** — Recommends section flow, visual weight distribution, and scroll depth management
7. **Designs CTA strategy** — Determines number of CTAs, placement, copy, and visual treatment
8. **Builds optimization roadmap** — Prioritizes A/B tests based on expected impact

The command delegates to the **cro-specialist** and **copywriter** agents, combining conversion rate expertise with persuasive writing.

## Important Notes

- **One page, one goal.** Every element on the page should drive toward a single conversion action. The agent will flag competing CTAs, navigation links, or distractions that split attention.
- **Traffic source matters.** A landing page for cold paid traffic needs more education than one for warm email subscribers. Specify your traffic source for tailored recommendations.
- **Copy does the heavy lifting.** Design matters, but most conversion issues are copy issues — unclear value proposition, wrong messaging hierarchy, or missing objection handling.
- **Test one thing at a time.** The optimization roadmap is prioritized so you can run tests sequentially and attribute results to specific changes.
- **Mobile-first design is assumed.** The recommendations account for mobile layout, thumb-friendly CTA placement, and reduced scroll tolerance on small screens.
- **Page speed affects conversion.** A one-second delay in load time can reduce conversions by 7%. The agent may flag asset optimization if relevant.
