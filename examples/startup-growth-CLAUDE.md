# CLAUDE.md — Early-Stage Startup (Growth Focus)

## Company Context

We are **Snapform**, an early-stage startup building an AI-powered form builder that converts 3x better than traditional forms. We just closed our seed round and are focused on hitting product-market fit and growing to 1,000 paying customers.

- **Product:** AI form builder — generates high-converting forms from a text description, optimizes fields and layout automatically
- **Stage:** Seed ($2.5M raised), 6 people total, 1 person on marketing (that's me)
- **Current metrics:** 2,800 free users, 180 paying customers, $14K MRR
- **Target:** 1,000 paying customers and $100K MRR in 12 months
- **Pricing:** Free tier (3 forms), Pro $29/mo, Team $79/mo

## Lean Marketing Context

I'm the only marketer. Every minute and dollar matters. Claude is my marketing team.

### What I need Claude to do:
- Be opinionated — don't give me 5 options, give me the best one
- Be fast — ship-quality drafts, not "starting points"
- Think in experiments — everything is a hypothesis to test
- Prioritize high-leverage activities (content that compounds, templates that convert, features that spread)
- Default to scrappy over polished

### What I don't need:
- Long strategy documents nobody will read
- Perfect copy that takes forever
- Enterprise marketing tactics (ABM, field marketing, analyst relations)

## Growth Strategy: PLG + Content

### Product-Led Growth
- Free tier is the top of funnel — optimize for signup and activation
- In-app prompts drive upgrades (usage limits, premium features)
- Built-in virality: forms display "Made with Snapform" badge on free tier
- Template gallery drives organic discovery and signups

### Content Marketing (Primary channel)
- **SEO blog posts:** Target "form builder" and adjacent keywords (2x/week)
- **Template gallery:** Pre-built form templates that rank for long-tail keywords
- **Comparison pages:** "Snapform vs Typeform", "Snapform vs Google Forms", etc.
- **Integration guides:** "How to connect Snapform to [tool]" for each integration
- **Use case pages:** One page per use case (lead gen, surveys, applications, quizzes)

### Other Channels
- **Twitter/X:** Founder personal brand, product updates, growth learnings (daily)
- **Product Hunt:** Major launches and feature releases
- **Indie Hackers / Hacker News:** Community participation, not promotion
- **Email:** Onboarding sequence (7 emails), monthly product updates
- **Partnerships:** Co-marketing with complementary tools (CRMs, email platforms)

## Growth Experiment Framework

Every marketing activity is framed as an experiment:

```
## Experiment: [Name]
**Hypothesis:** If we [action], then [expected outcome], because [reasoning].
**Metric:** [Primary metric to track]
**Timeline:** [Duration]
**Effort:** [Low / Medium / High]
**Expected impact:** [Low / Medium / High]
**Status:** [Planned / Running / Complete]
**Result:** [To be filled after experiment]
**Learnings:** [To be filled after experiment]
```

Prioritize experiments using ICE scoring:
- **Impact:** How much will this move the needle? (1-10)
- **Confidence:** How sure are we this will work? (1-10)
- **Ease:** How easy is this to implement? (1-10)

## Agent Preferences

- **copywriter** — Use for all content. Set tone to "conversational, confident, slightly playful." We sound like a smart friend, not a corporation.
- **seo-analyst** — Use before every blog post. Focus on low-competition, high-intent keywords. We can't compete on head terms yet.
- **cro-specialist** — Use for landing page reviews and signup flow optimization. Conversion rate is our most important metric.
- **product-marketer** — Use for comparison pages and positioning work. Keep it factual, not salesy.
- **analytics-interpreter** — Use weekly to review metrics and identify what's working.

## Brand Voice (Keep It Simple)

- Write like a human, not a brand
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs.
- Lead with the outcome ("Get 3x more responses" not "Our AI-powered form builder uses...")
- Be specific ("Takes 30 seconds" not "Quick and easy")
- Use "you" and "your" — it's a conversation
- OK to be a little playful, never corny
- No jargon, no buzzwords, no fluff

## Important Metrics to Track

- **North star:** Paid customers (currently 180, target 1,000)
- **Activation rate:** % of signups who create and publish a form (currently 34%)
- **Free-to-paid conversion:** Currently 6.4%, target 10%
- **MRR:** Currently $14K, target $100K
- **Blog traffic:** Currently 8K monthly visits, target 50K
- **CAC:** Must stay under $50 (current ~$35 via organic)
