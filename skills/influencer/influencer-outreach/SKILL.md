# Influencer Outreach

## When to Activate

Use this skill when identifying influencers for a campaign, evaluating potential creator partnerships, writing outreach emails or DMs, negotiating influencer deals, building a long-term creator relationship program, planning an influencer marketing campaign, or when someone asks to "find influencers," "build a creator program," or "partner with content creators." Also activate when evaluating whether influencer marketing is the right channel for a given goal.

## First Questions

Before starting influencer outreach, clarify:

1. **What is the campaign goal?** (awareness, traffic, leads, sales, content creation, social proof)
2. **What is the budget?** (determines which tiers of influencers are viable)
3. **Who is the target audience?** (demographics, interests, platforms — the influencer's audience must match)
4. **What platform(s)?** (Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, podcast, newsletter)
5. **What type of content?** (review, tutorial, unboxing, sponsored post, affiliate, brand ambassador)
6. **What is the timeline?** (one-off campaign, ongoing partnership, seasonal promotion)

## Core Rules

1. **Audience match over follower count.** A 10K-follower creator whose audience is exactly your target customer outperforms a 500K-follower creator whose audience is broadly "interested in business."
2. **Engagement rate is the first filter.** High followers with low engagement means bought followers, dead accounts, or irrelevant content. Check engagement before anything else.
3. **Authenticity is non-negotiable.** Influencers who promote anything for money damage both their credibility and yours. Look for creators who genuinely fit your brand.
4. **Pay fairly.** Underpaying creators leads to low-quality content, missed deadlines, and resentment. Fair compensation builds long-term relationships.
5. **Relationships beat transactions.** One-off sponsored posts have limited impact. Long-term partnerships where the creator genuinely uses and advocates for your product are 10x more valuable.
6. **Give creative freedom.** Creators know their audience better than you do. Provide guardrails (key messages, compliance requirements, brand guidelines) but let them create in their own voice.

## Influencer Tiers

| Tier | Follower Range | Engagement Rate | Typical Cost Per Post | Best For |
|------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|----------|
| Nano | 1K-10K | 4-8% | $50-500 | Hyper-local, niche audiences, authenticity, UGC creation |
| Micro | 10K-100K | 2-5% | $500-5,000 | Targeted reach, strong engagement, cost-effective campaigns |
| Mid-Tier | 100K-500K | 1.5-3% | $5,000-25,000 | Balanced reach and engagement, established credibility |
| Macro | 500K-1M | 1-2% | $25,000-75,000 | Broad reach, brand awareness, major product launches |
| Mega / Celebrity | 1M+ | 0.5-1.5% | $75,000-500,000+ | Mass awareness, mainstream cultural moments |

### Which Tier to Choose

- **Limited budget + niche audience**: Nano and micro. Partner with 10-20 nano creators for the cost of one macro. Higher total engagement.
- **Product launch + need reach**: Mid-tier to macro. Balance of visibility and credibility.
- **Brand awareness + mainstream audience**: Macro to mega. High reach but lower engagement rate.
- **UGC for ads**: Nano and micro. They create authentic-feeling content that performs well as paid ad creative.
- **Long-term ambassador program**: Micro to mid-tier. Affordable enough for ongoing relationships, engaged enough to drive results.

## Discovery Methods

### Platform Native Search
- Search relevant hashtags and keywords on the target platform.
- Explore "suggested creators" and "related accounts" features.
- Review who your target audience follows and engages with.
- Check who competitors are partnering with (look for #ad, #sponsored, #partner tags).

### Influencer Discovery Tools
- **CreatorIQ**: Enterprise-level discovery, vetting, and campaign management.
- **Upfluence**: Database search with audience demographics and engagement data.
- **Aspire (formerly AspireIQ)**: Discovery plus relationship management.
- **Modash**: Database of every creator with 1K+ followers across Instagram, TikTok, YouTube.
- **SparkToro**: Audience research that reveals which creators your target audience follows.
- **Heepsy**: Affordable influencer search with filters for location, category, engagement.

### Manual Discovery Methods
- **Hashtag analysis**: Search 10-15 relevant hashtags. Note creators who appear repeatedly with quality content.
- **Competitor analysis**: Review competitors' tagged posts, partnerships, and sponsored content.
- **Community mining**: Look in relevant subreddits, Facebook groups, Slack communities, and Discord servers for active, respected voices.
- **Conference speakers**: Industry events feature emerging thought leaders who often have engaged audiences.
- **Newsletter and podcast hosts**: Often overlooked but have highly engaged, high-trust audiences.
- **Customer base**: Your existing customers who create content may be your best micro-influencers.

## Evaluation Criteria

### Quantitative Assessment

| Metric | How to Evaluate | Red Flag Threshold |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Engagement Rate | (Likes + Comments) / Followers x 100 | Below 1% on Instagram, below 3% on TikTok |
| Follower Growth | Steady organic growth over 6+ months | Sudden spikes (likely bought followers) |
| Audience Demographics | Use tools or ask creator for media kit with audience data | Audience doesn't match your target market |
| Content Frequency | How often they post | Inactive for 2+ weeks without explanation |
| Views vs. Followers | Video views should be 10-30% of follower count | Consistently below 5% (dead audience) |

### Qualitative Assessment

| Factor | What to Look For | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Content Quality | Production value, storytelling, originality | Exclusively reposts, low effort, or copied content |
| Brand Fit | Values, aesthetic, and tone align with your brand | Promotes contradictory products or lifestyles |
| Authenticity | Genuine voice, honest opinions, personal stories | Every post is sponsored, no organic content |
| Audience Quality | Real comments with substance, genuine interaction | Generic comments ("nice!", emoji-only), bot patterns |
| Past Partnerships | Professional execution, positive brand associations | Excessive brand partnerships (more than 30% of content) |
| Controversy Risk | Scan last 6-12 months of content for issues | Inflammatory statements, legal issues, brand safety concerns |

### Red Flags: Fake Followers and Engagement

Signs of bought followers:
- Sudden follower spikes without corresponding content or viral moments
- High follower count but very low engagement (below 0.5%)
- Followers with no profile photos, no posts, or non-Latin usernames in bulk
- Comments that are generic, off-topic, or emoji-only
- Follower-to-following ratio near 1:1 (follow-for-follow behavior)

Signs of engagement pods:
- Same group of accounts commenting on every post
- Comments appear within seconds of posting
- Comments are suspiciously enthusiastic but vague
- High comment count but comments don't relate to the content

**Tools for detection:** HypeAuditor, Social Blade (growth patterns), manual spot-checking of followers and commenters.

## Outreach Email Templates

### Cold Pitch — Product Gifting

> Subject: Love your [specific content reference] — gift from [Brand]?
>
> Hi [Creator Name],
>
> I'm [Your name] from [Brand]. I've been following your content on [platform] for a while — your [specific post/video] about [topic] really resonated with our team.
>
> We make [product — one sentence description], and I think it'd be a great fit for your audience of [their audience description]. I'd love to send you [product] to try — no strings attached. If you love it and want to share it with your audience, amazing. If not, no pressure at all.
>
> Would you be open to receiving [product]? Happy to share more details.
>
> Best,
> [Your name]
> [Title, Brand]

### Collaboration Proposal — Paid Partnership

> Subject: Paid partnership opportunity — [Brand] x [Creator Name]
>
> Hi [Creator Name],
>
> I'm [Your name], [title] at [Brand]. We [one sentence about what you do and for whom].
>
> I'm reaching out because your content on [platform] aligns perfectly with our audience — particularly your [specific content reference]. We'd love to explore a paid collaboration.
>
> Here's what we're thinking:
> - **Content**: [type of content — e.g., 1 dedicated Instagram Reel + 2 Story frames]
> - **Topic**: [brief or theme — e.g., "Your honest experience using [product] for [use case]"]
> - **Timeline**: [target publish window]
> - **Compensation**: [budget range or "competitive rate based on your media kit"]
>
> We're flexible on format and want to work with your creative style, not against it. If this sounds interesting, I'd love to hop on a quick call to discuss.
>
> Could you share your media kit and rates?
>
> Best,
> [Your name]

### Ambassador Invitation — Long-Term

> Subject: [Creator Name] — join the [Brand] Ambassador Program?
>
> Hi [Creator Name],
>
> I'm [Your name] from [Brand]. You've been on our radar for a while — your [specific observation about their content or community] is exactly the kind of authentic voice we want to partner with long-term.
>
> We're building an ambassador program for creators who genuinely love [product/category] and want to grow alongside us. Here's what it looks like:
>
> **What you get:**
> - [Compensation — monthly retainer, commission, or per-post rate]
> - Free access to [product/features/events]
> - Early access to new launches and exclusive content
> - Co-marketing opportunities (features on our channels, case studies, events)
> - Direct line to our product team for feedback and feature requests
>
> **What we ask:**
> - [Content commitment — e.g., 2 posts per month minimum]
> - Authentic, in-your-voice content (we don't do scripts)
> - Feedback on our product and marketing (we actually listen)
>
> This is a [3-month / 6-month / 12-month] commitment with renewal based on mutual fit. Interested in learning more?
>
> Best,
> [Your name]

### Outreach Best Practices
- **Personalize every message.** Reference specific content they've created. Generic "we love your content" is transparent and ignored.
- **Keep it short.** Under 200 words for the first email. Details come later.
- **Lead with what's in it for them.** Not what you need from them.
- **Follow up once.** If no response after 5-7 days, send one follow-up. After that, move on.
- **Use their preferred channel.** Some creators prefer DMs, others email. Check their bio for contact preferences.
- **Don't lowball.** If your budget doesn't match their tier, don't insult them with an offer well below market rate.

## Negotiation Framework

### Key Terms to Negotiate

| Term | What It Covers | Standard Practice |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Deliverables | Number and type of content pieces | Be specific: "1 Instagram Reel (60-90 sec) + 3 Instagram Stories" |
| Usage Rights | Can the brand reuse the content? | Limited usage (brand's social) is standard. Extended usage (ads, website) costs 30-100% more |
| Exclusivity | Can the creator work with competitors? | Category exclusivity for the campaign duration is common. Extended exclusivity costs more |
| Timeline | Content creation and publishing schedule | Allow 2-4 weeks from brief to publish. Rush timelines cost more |
| Revisions | How many rounds of feedback | 1-2 rounds is standard. More than that signals a script, not a collaboration |
| Payment Terms | When and how the creator gets paid | 50% upfront + 50% on publish is common. Net-30 for established relationships |
| FTC Compliance | Disclosure requirements | Creator must clearly disclose (#ad, #sponsored, "Paid partnership with"). Non-negotiable |
| Approval Process | Who reviews content before it goes live | Brand reviews for accuracy and compliance, not creative direction |
| Performance Bonuses | Extra pay for exceeding targets | Bonus for exceeding engagement or conversion targets incentivizes effort |

### Pricing Negotiation

- **Start by asking for their rate card / media kit.** Let them anchor first.
- **If their rate is above budget:** Propose a smaller scope (fewer deliverables), longer-term deal (volume discount), or performance-based component (base + bonus).
- **If they're open to negotiation:** Offer value beyond cash — product, exposure, content repurposing rights, affiliate commissions.
- **Package deals:** Bundled multi-post deals are typically 15-25% cheaper per post than one-offs.
- **Affiliate hybrid:** Base fee + commission on sales gives both parties skin in the game.

## Red Flags During Outreach and Negotiation

| Red Flag | What It Signals | Action |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| Won't share audience demographics | May have mismatched or fake audience | Pass |
| Demands full payment upfront with no track record | Risk of non-delivery | Propose milestone-based payment |
| Refuses any form of disclosure | FTC compliance risk | Hard pass — legal liability |
| Wants full creative control with no review | Risk of off-brand or inaccurate content | Establish a light review process |
| Promotes a competitor the same week | No regard for brand partnerships | Discuss exclusivity or pass |
| Response time is weeks | Unreliable communication | Consider carefully — may miss deadlines |
| Inflated metrics vs. third-party data | Dishonest about performance | Verify with tools, then pass if confirmed |

## Relationship Management for Long-Term Partnerships

### Building the Relationship
- **Treat creators as partners, not vendors.** Include them in product feedback, invite them to company events, ask their opinion on campaigns.
- **Pay on time, every time.** Late payment is the fastest way to lose a creator relationship.
- **Share performance data.** Show them how their content performed. Creators want to know they're making an impact.
- **Celebrate their wins.** If they hit a milestone, launch something new, or receive recognition — congratulate them publicly and privately.
- **Give them first access.** New product launches, features, announcements — let ambassadors know before the public.

### Ambassador Program Structure
- **Tiered program**: Entry tier (new partners), growth tier (proven performers), elite tier (top ambassadors with expanded benefits).
- **Clear expectations**: Monthly content commitments, response time for briefs, disclosure compliance.
- **Regular check-ins**: Monthly or quarterly calls to discuss performance, feedback, and upcoming plans.
- **Community building**: Connect ambassadors with each other. A private Slack channel or annual meetup builds loyalty.
- **Renewal and off-boarding**: Set clear contract terms. If the partnership isn't working, end it respectfully with a clear off-boarding process.

### Creator Relationship CRM
Track for each influencer:
- Contact info and preferred communication channel
- Platform(s) and follower count (updated quarterly)
- Engagement rate (updated quarterly)
- Past collaborations (dates, content type, performance)
- Rate / compensation history
- Audience demographics
- Relationship notes (preferences, feedback, personal details)
- Contract status and renewal dates

## Quality Gate

Before launching influencer outreach:

- [ ] Campaign goal is defined and measurable (awareness, traffic, leads, sales)
- [ ] Target audience is defined and matches the influencer's audience (not just the influencer's persona)
- [ ] Influencer evaluation includes both quantitative (engagement, demographics) and qualitative (brand fit, authenticity) criteria
- [ ] Fake follower and engagement pod checks have been conducted
- [ ] Outreach is personalized with specific content references (not template-blasted)
- [ ] Compensation is fair and appropriate for the influencer's tier and deliverables
- [ ] Contract terms cover deliverables, usage rights, exclusivity, timeline, and FTC compliance
- [ ] FTC disclosure requirements are explicitly communicated and non-negotiable
- [ ] A tracking mechanism is in place (UTMs, promo codes, affiliate links) to measure performance
- [ ] The plan accounts for long-term relationship building, not just one-off transactions
