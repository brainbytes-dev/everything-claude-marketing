# Launch Playbook

## When to Activate

- You have a GTM strategy and now need the tactical execution plan
- Launch day is approaching and you need an hour-by-hour playbook
- Managing the cross-functional coordination of a product launch
- Planning pre-launch activities (beta, early access, press prep)
- Orchestrating post-launch amplification and measurement
- Running a launch retrospective to improve future launches

## First Questions

1. What is the launch tier? (T1 major, T2 moderate, T3 minor — see go-to-market skill)
2. What is the launch date and is it firm or flexible?
3. Is there a press embargo or exclusive arrangement?
4. Is there a beta or early access program running pre-launch?
5. What channels will be activated on launch day?
6. Who is the launch owner responsible for cross-functional coordination?
7. What is the go/no-go criteria? (What must be true for launch to proceed?)
8. What is the rollback plan if something goes wrong?

## Pre-Launch Phase (T-30 to T-0)

### T-30 to T-21: Foundation

**Product Readiness**
- [ ] Feature complete and in QA
- [ ] Critical bugs resolved, known issues documented
- [ ] Performance and load testing completed
- [ ] Rollback plan documented and tested

**Messaging and Positioning**
- [ ] Positioning finalized (use product-positioning skill)
- [ ] Key messages documented (3-5 messages with supporting proof points)
- [ ] Messaging tested with internal stakeholders and 3-5 external customers
- [ ] Competitive positioning updated

**Beta / Early Access Program**
- [ ] Beta cohort identified (15-30 users for meaningful feedback)
- [ ] Beta onboarding materials prepared
- [ ] Feedback collection mechanism in place (surveys, interviews, in-app feedback)
- [ ] Beta success criteria defined (activation rate, NPS, feature usage)
- [ ] Beta user NDA or communication guidelines (if pre-public launch)

### T-21 to T-14: Content Creation

**Written Content**
- [ ] Blog post drafted and reviewed
- [ ] Landing page copy written and designed
- [ ] Customer email copy drafted (segment by: all customers, relevant segment, beta participants)
- [ ] Internal announcement drafted
- [ ] Help documentation written
- [ ] FAQ document prepared
- [ ] Case study or customer quote secured (from beta participants)

**Visual and Video Content**
- [ ] Product screenshots / GIFs captured
- [ ] Demo video produced (60-90 seconds, clear value demonstration)
- [ ] Social media images/videos created (platform-specific formats)
- [ ] Presentation deck updated for sales team
- [ ] Email header/banner designed

**Press Materials**
- [ ] Press release drafted (see press-releases skill)
- [ ] Media kit prepared (logo, screenshots, exec bios, fact sheet)
- [ ] Spokesperson briefed and available on launch day
- [ ] Press embargo or exclusive arranged (if T1 launch)
- [ ] Media pitch emails drafted (see media-pitching skill)

### T-14 to T-7: Enablement and Alignment

**Sales Enablement**
- [ ] Sales training session scheduled and delivered
- [ ] Battlecards updated
- [ ] Demo script and environment prepared
- [ ] Pricing and packaging confirmed with sales leadership
- [ ] Target account list for launch outreach identified
- [ ] Objection handling document updated
- [ ] Commission / incentive structure communicated (if new product)

**Customer Success Enablement**
- [ ] CS team trained on new feature/product
- [ ] Proactive outreach list for high-value accounts prepared
- [ ] Migration or upgrade path documented
- [ ] Success metrics for customers using new feature defined

**Support Enablement**
- [ ] Support team trained
- [ ] Knowledge base articles published (hidden until launch)
- [ ] Escalation paths defined
- [ ] Expected ticket volume estimated and staffing adjusted

**Cross-Functional Sync**
- [ ] Launch readiness meeting with all stakeholders
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities confirmed for launch day
- [ ] Communication channels established (Slack channel, war room)
- [ ] Contingency plans reviewed (what if product isn't ready? What if press leaks early?)

### T-7 to T-1: Final Preparation

**Technical Readiness**
- [ ] Landing pages live (unlisted/gated until launch)
- [ ] Email sequences loaded, tested (send test to 3+ devices/clients)
- [ ] Social media posts drafted and scheduled
- [ ] Ad campaigns built and ready to activate
- [ ] Analytics tracking verified (events, goals, UTMs)
- [ ] In-product announcement configured
- [ ] Changelog entry prepared

**Press Readiness**
- [ ] Embargo materials sent to journalists (if applicable)
- [ ] Follow-up calls with key journalists scheduled
- [ ] Spokesperson final prep and rehearsal

**Internal Readiness**
- [ ] Final go/no-go meeting (T-1 or T-2)
- [ ] Launch day playbook distributed to all team members
- [ ] War room / monitoring channel set up
- [ ] On-call schedule confirmed for launch day

### Go/No-Go Criteria
Launch proceeds if ALL of these are true:
- [ ] Product is stable and passes QA
- [ ] All critical launch assets are approved and ready
- [ ] Sales and support teams are trained
- [ ] No known issues that would significantly harm the customer experience
- [ ] Press/media plans are confirmed
- [ ] Launch owner gives final approval

## Launch Day Playbook

### Hour-by-Hour Plan (Tier 1 Launch)

```
T-1 HOUR (Pre-Launch)
- War room / Slack channel active
- All team members confirm readiness
- Final product check (is everything working?)
- Verify landing pages, emails, and social posts are ready

T-0 (LAUNCH)
- Product / feature goes live
- Blog post published
- Landing page goes public
- Press release distributed / embargo lifts
- Internal announcement sent (Slack, email)
- First social media post goes live

T+1 HOUR
- Email sent to primary customer segment
- Monitor: Website traffic, sign-ups, social mentions
- Check for technical issues (monitoring dashboards)
- Respond to first social media comments

T+2 HOURS
- Second social media post (different angle/platform)
- Monitor press coverage — share and amplify any publications
- Check: Email delivery, open rates, early click data
- Sales team begins outreach to target accounts

T+4 HOURS
- Third social media post
- Address any customer questions or issues
- First internal update: early metrics and sentiment
- Amplify any customer posts or press coverage

T+6 HOURS
- Review all channels: website, social, email, press, support
- Adjust paid amplification based on early performance
- Send secondary email (different segment or different angle)
- Flag any issues requiring immediate attention

END OF DAY
- Comprehensive launch day metrics report
- Social media recap (mentions, sentiment, top posts)
- Press coverage summary
- Support ticket summary
- Internal debrief with launch team
```

### Social Media Cadence (Launch Day)

| Time | Platform | Content |
|------|----------|---------|
| T+0 | All platforms | Main announcement with link |
| T+1h | X/Twitter | Thread with details / features |
| T+2h | LinkedIn | Long-form post from CEO or product lead |
| T+3h | Instagram/TikTok | Visual demo / short video |
| T+4h | X/Twitter | Retweet/amplify press coverage or customer reactions |
| T+6h | LinkedIn | Share press coverage or customer quote |
| T+8h | All platforms | "In case you missed it" recap post |

### Launch Day Monitoring

**What to Monitor**
- Website traffic (real-time analytics)
- Sign-up / conversion rates
- Social media mentions and sentiment
- Press coverage (Google News, social, direct monitoring)
- Support ticket volume and themes
- Product uptime and performance
- Email metrics (delivery, opens, clicks)

**Escalation Triggers**
- Product is down or experiencing errors → Immediately pause marketing, activate engineering
- Negative press coverage → Activate crisis communication (see crisis-communication skill)
- Social media backlash → Assess severity, respond per social media guidelines
- Zero press coverage → Activate follow-up pitch sequence to Tier 2 media

## Post-Launch Phase (T+1 to T+30)

### T+1 to T+3 (Immediate Post-Launch)

**Performance Tracking**
- [ ] Compile full launch day metrics (traffic, sign-ups, coverage, social, support)
- [ ] Compare actual performance vs. targets
- [ ] Identify top-performing channels and content
- [ ] Identify underperforming areas for immediate adjustment

**Amplification**
- [ ] Share press coverage on social media and in email newsletter
- [ ] Send follow-up email to non-openers with adjusted subject line
- [ ] Boost top-performing social posts with paid
- [ ] Post in relevant communities (Reddit, Hacker News, Product Hunt, Slack groups, Discord)
- [ ] Ask beta users and champions to share their experience

**Content Generation**
- [ ] Collect and share early customer reactions and quotes
- [ ] Begin creating content from launch learnings (lessons learned post, behind-the-scenes)
- [ ] Identify user-generated content to amplify

### T+3 to T+14 (Sustained Amplification)

**Ongoing Activities**
- [ ] Continue social media posting cadence (2-3x per week referencing the launch)
- [ ] Publish additional content (how-to guides, use case posts, deep-dive features)
- [ ] Run a customer webinar or live demo
- [ ] Follow up with journalists who didn't cover the launch with new angles
- [ ] Sales outreach to target accounts referencing launch momentum

**Iteration Based on Data**
- [ ] A/B test landing page elements based on launch traffic data
- [ ] Adjust paid targeting based on conversion data
- [ ] Refine messaging based on customer feedback and social conversation
- [ ] Address product issues surfaced during launch

### T+14 to T+30 (Transition to Steady State)

**Measurement**
- [ ] 30-day launch performance report
- [ ] Cohort analysis: How do launch-acquired users retain vs. baseline?
- [ ] Channel attribution: Which channels drove the most valuable customers?
- [ ] Cost analysis: What was the cost per acquisition for launch activities?

**Case Studies**
- [ ] Identify 2-3 early adopters willing to be case studies
- [ ] Begin case study interviews and production
- [ ] Capture quantitative results from early adopters

**Handoff**
- [ ] Transition from launch mode to ongoing marketing
- [ ] Ensure all launch content is findable and reusable
- [ ] Update product marketing materials with launch learnings

## Launch Communication Templates

### Internal Announcement
```
Subject: [Product/Feature Name] is LIVE

Team,

I'm excited to share that [product/feature] launched today.

WHAT: [1-2 sentence description]
WHO IT'S FOR: [Target customer/segment]
WHY IT MATTERS: [Key value proposition]

KEY LINKS:
- Blog post: [link]
- Landing page: [link]
- Help docs: [link]
- Sales deck: [link]

HOW YOU CAN HELP:
- Share the blog post on your personal social channels
- Forward to anyone who might benefit
- Flag any customer questions to [person]

If customers or prospects ask about this, direct them to [link]
or connect them with [person].

[Name]
```

### Customer Email
```
Subject: [Benefit-oriented headline, not feature name]

Hi [Name],

[1-2 sentences connecting to their problem/need]

Today we're launching [product/feature] — [what it does in
one sentence].

Here's what this means for you:
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

[CTA button: Try it now / See how it works / Learn more]

[Optional: "Here's what [beta customer name] said: 'Quote.'"]

Questions? Reply to this email or [book a demo/visit help center].

[Signature]
```

### Blog Post Structure
```
HEADLINE: [Benefit-focused, not feature-focused]

INTRO (2-3 sentences):
- The problem this solves
- Why it matters now

THE ANNOUNCEMENT (1 paragraph):
- What you're launching
- Key capabilities

WHY WE BUILT THIS (2-3 paragraphs):
- Customer problem in their words
- How you approached solving it
- What makes your approach different

HOW IT WORKS (3-5 sections with screenshots):
- Feature/capability 1
- Feature/capability 2
- Feature/capability 3

EARLY RESULTS / CUSTOMER QUOTE:
- Beta results or customer testimonial

GETTING STARTED:
- How to try it (CTA)
- Pricing info if relevant
- Link to docs

WHAT'S NEXT:
- Roadmap tease (build anticipation)
```

## Launch Retrospective Template

**1. Results vs. Goals**
| Metric | Goal | Actual | Status |
|--------|------|--------|--------|
| Sign-ups | X | X | Met / Exceeded / Missed |
| Activation rate | X% | X% | ... |
| Revenue / Pipeline | $X | $X | ... |
| Press coverage | X articles | X articles | ... |
| Social reach | X impressions | X impressions | ... |

**2. What Went Well**
- [Thing 1 — be specific about what and why]
- [Thing 2]
- [Thing 3]

**3. What Didn't Go Well**
- [Thing 1 — be specific about impact and root cause]
- [Thing 2]
- [Thing 3]

**4. What We Learned**
- [Learning 1 — what will we do differently next time?]
- [Learning 2]
- [Learning 3]

**5. Action Items for Next Launch**
- [ ] [Improvement 1] — Owner: [Name]
- [ ] [Improvement 2] — Owner: [Name]
- [ ] [Improvement 3] — Owner: [Name]

**6. Thank Yous**
- Acknowledge specific contributions from cross-functional team members.

## Quality Gate

Before launch:
- [ ] Has go/no-go meeting happened with all stakeholders?
- [ ] Is the product stable and performing well?
- [ ] Are all launch assets created, reviewed, and approved?
- [ ] Are all cross-functional teams trained and ready?
- [ ] Is the launch day playbook distributed with hour-by-hour responsibilities?
- [ ] Is the monitoring/war room set up?
- [ ] Is there a rollback or contingency plan?
- [ ] Are success metrics and targets defined?

After launch:
- [ ] Have you compiled launch day metrics within 24 hours?
- [ ] Is there an amplification plan for T+1 to T+14?
- [ ] Are you collecting customer feedback for iteration?
- [ ] Have you identified early case study candidates?
- [ ] Is a retrospective scheduled for T+14?
